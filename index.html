Get That Water!

Get That Water! is a critical design board game that shows the uneven power dynamic between corporations, and average person trying to seek water. In the game, one player is a Corporation and the other is a Water Seeker. Each turn, the player picks up an action card according to the role they are playing as. Action cards illustrate real life water-issue scenarios like corporations polluting water sources, corporations buying out water sources, and in turn people no longer being able to access water. Additionally, there are four water source spaces; tap water, natural spring water, lake water, and ocean water that players can gain control of. The Water Seeker is never able to win the game in order to demonstrate the real life power dynamic between corporations and average citizens.

My Agony

My Agony is a conversation simulator game made in Phaser.js where you play as a women who recieves countless sexist comments from a man when she shows interest in applying to be CEO of a technology company. To make the conversations as realistic as possible, I based the dialogue off of testimonials from women in the industry. My efforts and research in creating My Agony led to me being awarded with an Outstandinf Research Award at the James J. Whalen Academic Symposium.
