Around the Web

Star Dog

Star Dog is a 2D side scrolling shooter game, centers around a super powered alien dog that has to rescue his human companion from an evil corporation who wants to take him back as test subject. The game is made in the Javascript framework, Phaser.js. My role in the on going project was to help design the Object Oriented Engine as well as make the sound effects and the music.

Hike That

Prototype for a mobile application that suggests users hikes based on their location and skill level. In addition, users can save their favorite hikes in a favorites section. Each hike also has a list of recommended gear and the weather on the for that day. Hike That was designed using paper prototyping, usability testing, mocking through sketch, and using invision for the interactions.

Perception Slider

Perception is an image slider that explores how people percieve you versus how you actually feel in college. For this project, I decided to visualize what is typically thought of as the two most stressful years in college, freshman and senior year, since it would be the most relatable. I created all the graphics in Adobe Illustrator and programmed the slider using the JavaScript library, Phaser.js.

My Agony

My Agony is a conversation simulator game made in Phaser.js where you play as a women who recieves countless sexist comments from a man when she shows interest in applying to be CEO of a technology company. To make the conversations as realistic as possible, I based the dialogue off of testimonials from women in the industry. My efforts and research in creating My Agony lead to me being awarded with an Outstandinf Research Award at the James J. Whalen Academic Symposium.

Interactive Pride Flag

I made an interactive Pride Flag with the goal of spreading LGBTQ history, which is not talked about much at all. Each stripe of the flag is clickable and shows what each colored stripe on the flag means. All the graphics are made using Adobe Illustrator and the interactive web element is programmed using standard web technologies such as HTML, CSS, Bootstrap4, and JQuery.

Time Out

Time Out is a safety app for the Pebble Smart Watch made at WiCHacks that allows the user to send a discrete message to an emergency contact based on the severity of the situation. Time Out was made in the language C and using the Pebble Development Environment while also incorporating the Sendgrid API. Time Out was awarded Best Hardware Hack by Liberty Mutual at WiCHacks
